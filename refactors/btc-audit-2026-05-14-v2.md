# BTC Signals + Backtesting — Evidence-Based Audit v2
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Window analyzed:** 2026-04-13 → 2026-05-14 (31 days, 559 BTC signals, 555 confirmed, 559 closed)
**Data source:** MongoDB `trades` collection — verified 559/559 parity with `trades.json` (zero ID/field drift, 0 missing values on key fields).
**Methodology:** Every finding is classified into one of three tiers — **Defect** (provable from code, docs, or git history alone), **Open question** (resolution requires user intent), **Statistical hypothesis** (signed effect with Wilson 95% CI and Fisher exact p-value, n-stratified). No claim is made without one of these grounds. Where data is too small or confounded for confidence, the finding is filed as **insufficient evidence**.

---

## Methodology notes
- **Sample is single-regime**: BTC was in a broad uptrend across the window. Findings are conditional on that regime; out-of-regime confidence is unknown.
- **A confirmation-semantics fix shipped 2026-04-26** (commit `4f3a944d`). Pre-fix: 120 trades (2026-04-13 → 04-26). Post-fix: 439 trades (04-26 → 05-14). Statistical analysis uses the post-fix subset where commitment to current semantics matters; otherwise pooled with the caveat noted.
- **Confidence intervals**: Wilson score (binomial). **Significance tests**: two-sided Fisher exact on 2×2.
- **Effect-size threshold for actionable**: |lift| ≥ 10pp **AND** p < 0.05 **AND** n ≥ 50 per cell.

## TIER 1 — DEFECTS (provable; recommend fix)

### D1. `updateOutcomes` runs on unconfirmed trades, producing −1R losses on positions that were never entered
**Code:** [scripts/trigger-check.js:1631](scripts/trigger-check.js:1631) — `open = trades.filter(t => t.outcome === null)` does not gate on `confirmed`.
**Authoritative design intent:** BACKTESTING.md states "An unconfirmed trade means the level was approached but **price never convincingly broke through with order flow — the trade setup was seen but the entry condition was never met**." A position that was never entered cannot produce a −1R loss.
**Data evidence:** 4 trades in the current dataset have `confirmed:false, outcome:'stop', pnlR:-1`. Three are the same supply zone (73773–73400) fired 2026-04-13 at 19:40 / 20:50 / 22:00; none crossed entry; all logged as stops.
**Impact:** small in absolute terms (−4R) but the bug spreads further once D2 is fixed — currently many "unconfirmed" trades are later retroactively confirmed via D2.
**Fix:** add `t.confirmed === true` to the `open` filter, or assign `outcome: 'unentered', pnlR: 0` on stop-hit-without-entry.
**Confidence:** ★★★ definitional.

### D2. Confirmation has no time-window cap; trades are stamped "confirmed" *after they have already closed*
**Code:** [scripts/trigger-check.js:1486–1534](scripts/trigger-check.js:1486). The `unconfirmed` filter is `!t.confirmed` (no upper time bound). Walks every 30M bar after `firedAt` until one closes beyond `entry`. There is no filter on `t.closedAt`.
**Authoritative design intent:** BACKTESTING.md silent on a time cap. However, the design says confirmation "is the entry trigger." An entry trigger that fires *after* the trade has already taken its outcome cannot be the entry trigger by definition. The intent contradicts the implementation.
**Data evidence (smoking gun):**
- Of 438 post-fix confirmed trades: **65 have `confirmedAt > closedAt`** — confirmation landed AFTER outcome was already recorded.
- **52 of those 65 are stops** (now retroactively counted as "confirmed losses").
- Worst case: trade fired 2026-04-27 04:30, stopped 30 minutes later, marked "confirmed" 2026-05-02 21:30 — a **136.5-hour** lag between close and confirmation.
- Of the 53 SLOW (≥1h) confirms, **44/53 (83%) have `confirmedAt > closedAt`**.

**Statistical impact:**
| Cohort | n | Win rate | 95% CI | Avg R |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| FAST confirm (<1h, post-fix) | 385 | **75.6%** | [71.3–79.9] | +1.31 |
| SLOW confirm (≥1h, post-fix) | 53 | **32.1%** | [21.2–45.5] | −0.04 |
| All post-fix confirmed (current state) | 438 | 70.3% | [65.9–74.4] | +1.14 |

Fisher exact two-sided **p = 7.7 × 10⁻¹⁰**. The fast/slow split is real, not noise.
**Fix:** cap confirmation latency. Mechanically: `if ((bar.time - signalTs) > MAX_CONFIRM_SECONDS) continue` inside the bar loop, and stop walking bars once exceeded. Recommended cap: 1 hour (≈ 2 × 30M bars). Even within the 0.5–1h band, win rate is 11.8% — so a 30-minute (1-bar) cap is defensible too.
**Confidence:** ★★★ provable. Logical (confirm-after-close), documentary (design intent), and statistical (Fisher p ≪ 0.001).

### D3. Outcome resolver contradicts its own author's stated rule on stop-vs-TP same-bar ambiguity
**Code:** [scripts/trigger-check.js:1684–1720](scripts/trigger-check.js:1684).
- Block reads `if (stopHit && !tp1Hit) {stop} else if (tp3Hit) {tp3} else if (tp2Hit) {tp2} else if (tp1Hit) {tp1} else if (stopHit) {stop}`.
- The terminal `else if (stopHit)` at line 1700 and 1718 is **unreachable** (if `stopHit && tp1Hit` and we reach the `tp3Hit` branch, one of the TP branches always fires first).
- **Effect:** on a bar where price wicked through both stop and a TP, TP wins.
**Authoritative design intent — THREE corroborating sources say stop wins:**
1. Code comment at [trigger-check.js:1620–1621](scripts/trigger-check.js:1620): *"On a bar where BOTH stop and a TP are crossed (a big candle), stop wins — conservative but honest. This prevents phantom TP hits on stop-out candles."*
2. Author's commit message for the function (commit `6a93d0dd`, 2026-04-15): *"stops and TPs are detected with correct intrabar order (**stop wins on ambiguous same-bar crossings**)"*.
3. BACKTESTING.md line 68: *"if bar.low <= stop AND bar.high >= tp1 → LOSS (same bar — **stop wins, conservative**)"*.
4. docs/performance-tracking.md line 51: *"**same-bar ambiguity: stop wins, conservative**"*.

This is not a judgment call — the author intended stop to win, then introduced the unreachable branch. The code as written silently does the opposite.
**Fix:** restructure to `if (stopHit) {stop} else if (tp3Hit) ... else if (tp2Hit) ... else if (tp1Hit) ...`. Match the documented rule. Remove the dead `else if (stopHit)` branch.
**Confidence:** ★★★ documentary + reachability proof.

### D4. The mid-bar fill model is provably optimistic; TP outcomes show wick-bias signature
**Code:** Same block as D3. When a stop is NOT hit on a bar, `tp3Hit` is checked first, then `tp2Hit`, then `tp1Hit`. The R-multiple recorded is the largest TP reached by the bar's *high* (long) or *low* (short), not by the bar's close.
**Diagnostic:**
| Outcome | Closed within first 30M bar (≤30 min after fire) | % |
|---|---:|---:|
| TP3 | **15 / 17** | **88%** |
| TP2 | 57 / 62 | 92% |
| TP1 | 143 / 212 | 67% |
| Stop | 35 / 94 | 37% |
*(post-fix clean cohort, n=385)*

If the strategy's TP3 target is several R away, a 88% rate of TP3 hitting in the first 30M bar is implausible from genuine multi-bar holds. The signature is consistent with intrabar wicks brushing the TP3 level rather than honest exits to TP3.
**Status:** **defect or design omission**. The code's fill model is optimistic; the docs are silent on what fill model was intended. The fix is two-fold:
  - If wick-fill is intended (matches a market-order TP): document it as the convention.
  - If close-based fill is intended (matches a more realistic outcome): change to `bar.close >= tpN` and weigh the R-impact.
**Confidence:** ★★★ for the diagnostic; the **fix is an open question** for the user (see Q1).

### D5. Setup probability constants drift from spec; observed values diverge by 14–22pp
**Code:** [scripts/trigger-check.js:910–914](scripts/trigger-check.js:910):
```
A — Full Confluence: 70
B — Partial Confluence: 60
C — Low Confluence: 48
```
**Source-of-truth (spec, strategies/smc-setups.md):** Setup A base ~62%, Setup B base ~52%, Setup C base ~70%, with a per-criterion weighted-delta formula (CVD +7/-11, OI +6/-9, etc.) and clamp to [28%, 91%]. The code does NOT implement the weighted formula; it uses three hardcoded tier constants instead.
**Data evidence (calibration on clean post-fix cohort, n=385):**
| Published | Observed | 95% CI | Δ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 48% (Setup C) | **65.0%** | [56.7–72.4] | **+17.0pp** |
| 60% (Setup B) | **78.6%** | [72.3–83.7] | **+18.6pp** |
| 70% (Setup A) | **92.3%** | [81.8–97.0] | **+22.3pp** |

All three published probabilities lie outside their observed 95% CIs (a stricter test than significance). The Discord-published probabilities understate actual win rate by 17–22 percentage points. (Full-window pooled values: A 84.9%, B 73.9%, C 62.7% — all 95% CIs exclude the published value.)
**Confidence:** ★★★ on the contradiction. The exact recalibration is a tuning recommendation (T1) because the sample is single-regime.

### D7. `MAX_INVALIDATION_ALERTS = 3` cap is actually 6

### D8. `weeklyTrend` factor has zero discriminatory power — always passes or unavailable
**Data:** 559 trades — 389 have `factors.weeklyTrend = null` (analyseWeeklyTrend returned 'neutral' or unavailable), 170 have `true` (aligned with trade direction), **0 have `false` (counter-trend)**. The system never signals counter-trend.
**Code path:** [scripts/trigger-check.js:502–512](scripts/trigger-check.js:502). `analyseWeeklyTrend()` uses 5 closes and emits 'uptrend' / 'downtrend' / 'neutral'. Returned 'neutral' or null on 70% of trades in this window. When non-neutral, the `aligned` check at [line 846](scripts/trigger-check.js:846) returned true on 100% of cases — but that's because the system simply never fires counter-trend in practice (BTC's weekly trend was 'uptrend' for the whole window and the system fires longs preferentially → always aligned).
**Effect:** factor is informationless in current data — Fisher test cannot be run (zero cells).
**Recommendation:** either log raw `analyseWeeklyTrend()` output for several weeks and re-evaluate, or drop the factor from the criteria score. Currently it's a free pass tied to the long-bias regime.
**Confidence:** ★★★ data-zero on the failure cell.

### D9. `lib/lock.js` is not used by `trigger-check.js`
**Code:** No `require('./lib/lock')` in trigger-check.js. The Sonnet 4.6 audit (refactors/recent-analysis.md, item IV) called this out 9 days ago; status unchanged.
**Confidence:** ★★★ static analysis. Note: collision frequency depends on whether a CDP poll exceeds 10 min — not observed-rare but worth fixing for correctness, not performance.

## TIER 2 — OPEN QUESTIONS (need user intent)

### Q1. What fill model should the resolver use?
- **Optimistic wick** (current code, undocumented): TP_N fires if `bar.high >= tp_N` even mid-bar.
- **Close-based**: only when `bar.close >= tp_N`. More realistic for limit orders that aren't actually placed.
- **Stop-first / TP-second by intrabar order**: would need tick data we don't have.

This is a strategic choice. Recommend documenting it explicitly once chosen. The fix interacts with D4.

### Q2. Should `mtf-analyze.js`'s weighted probability formula or `trigger-check.js`'s tier system be canonical?
**Today they disagree.** `!analyze` gives one number; cron alerts give another. The spec (smc-setups.md) describes the weighted formula. The code only implements the tier system.

### Q3. Is the 1-hour cooldown intended to be uniform, or should it lengthen after a stop on the same zone?
**Evidence prompting the question:** The three 2026-04-13 supply-zone shorts fired at 19:40 / 20:50 / 22:00 — exactly outside the 1h cooldown — all stopped. No same-zone stops fired re-trigger in the post-fix data, but the policy choice is unclear.

### Q4. Are unconfirmed-but-stopped signals a real trading state we want to log, or should they be filtered from the dataset?
Today they're written to `trades.json` with `pnlR:-1`. Per BACKTESTING.md they represent "level approached but entry not triggered." Decide whether to keep them as `outcome:'unentered'` (D1 fix variant) or drop them entirely.
