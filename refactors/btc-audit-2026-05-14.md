## Critical bugs

### C1 ‚Äî `updateOutcomes` runs on unconfirmed trades; produces phantom ‚àí1R losses
[scripts/trigger-check.js:1631](scripts/trigger-check.js:1631) ‚Äî `open = trades.filter(t => t.outcome === null)` does not gate on `confirmed`.
A signal fires, price never reaches `entry`, but a 30M bar later hits `stop` (the alert's invalidation level). Outcome is logged as `stop` with `pnlR=-1` even though no real entry occurred.
**Evidence:** 4 trades in current data have `confirmed:false, outcome:'stop'`. Three of them are the same supply zone (73773‚Äì73400) fired three times on 2026-04-13 at 19:40 / 20:50 / 22:00 ‚Äî all stopped before crossing entry. They pollute the "ALL SIGNALS" track in weekly-report.
**Fix:** gate `updateOutcomes` filter on `t.confirmed === true`, OR add `outcome: 'unconfirmed-stop'` with `pnlR: 0`. Either way, never count ‚àí1R on a trade you never entered.

### C2 ‚Äî Confirmation has no time window cap; stale "confirmations" are 100% losers
[scripts/trigger-check.js:1486‚Äì1534](scripts/trigger-check.js:1486) (`checkConfirmation`). Filter is `unconfirmed = trades.filter(t => !t.confirmed)`; walks ALL 30M bars after `firedAt`. No bound on how late confirmation can land.
**Evidence ‚Äî latency vs outcome (confirmed trades, n=555):**

| Latency band | n   | wins | stops | wr     | avgR  |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0‚Äì0.5h | 382 | 297 | 85  | 77.7%  | +1.37 |
| 0.5‚Äì1h | 97  | 80  | 17  | 82.5%  | +1.69 |
| **1‚Äì2h**   | **26**  | **7**   | **19**  | **26.9%**  | **‚àí0.46** |
| **2‚Äì6h**   | **29**  | **9**   | **20**  | **31.0%**  | **+0.19** |
| **6‚Äì24h**  | **7**   | **1**   | **6**   | **14.3%**  | **‚àí0.64** |
| **24h+**   | **14**  | **0**   | **14**  | **0.0%**   | **‚àí1.00** |

A 1h cap on confirmation moves the "confirmed signals" track from 71.5% wr ‚Üí **78.7% wr (377/479)**, with avgR rising correspondingly. The 76 trades that confirm after >1h are zombies ‚Äî typically already stopped, then briefly poking back across entry days later.
**Fix:** in `checkConfirmation`, skip bars where `bar.time - signalTs > 60 * 60` (or whatever cap matches your trade horizon ‚Äî TP1 avg time-to-close is 0.7h so 1h is generous).

### C3 ‚Äî Outcome resolver disagrees with its own comment about stop-vs-TP on the same bar
[scripts/trigger-check.js:1620‚Äì1621](scripts/trigger-check.js:1620) comment: *"On a bar where BOTH stop and a TP are crossed, stop wins ‚Äî conservative but honest."*
[scripts/trigger-check.js:1691‚Äì1703](scripts/trigger-check.js:1691) implementation: `if (stopHit && !tp1Hit)` then falls through to `tp3Hit / tp2Hit / tp1Hit` ‚Äî TP wins whenever any TP was reached. The trailing `else if (stopHit)` at line 1700/1718 is **dead code** (the prior `tp1Hit` branch always fires first).
On an ambiguous wick bar, the trade is recorded at the **highest TP touched**, not as a stop. Combined with C5 below, R-multiples may be systematically inflated.
**Fix:** decide the rule and align both. The conservative version is `if (stopHit) outcome='stop'; else if (tp3Hit) ... else if (tp2Hit) ... else if (tp1Hit) ...`. Then drop the dead branch.

### C4 ‚Äî Outcome resolver assigns the *highest* TP reached within a bar (look-ahead at the wick)
Same block, [scripts/trigger-check.js:1684‚Äì1720](scripts/trigger-check.js:1684). It checks `bar.high >= tp3` before `tp2` before `tp1`. If the wick touched tp3 even momentarily, the trade is closed at tp3 regardless of how the bar closed. This is the optimistic-fill model.
**Symptom:** TP3 closes in 0.7h avg, same as TP1, and faster than TP2 (0.5h) ‚Äî implausible if TP3 were a genuine multi-hour ride. The pattern is consistent with single-bar wicks rather than honest exits.
**Fix:** decide on a fill model (e.g. use `bar.close`, or `min(tp_n, bar.close)` for the realized side, or require a separate bar close beyond TP). At minimum, document the optimistic-fill bias so weekly-report disclaims it.

### C5 ‚Äî Published probability is wrong by 14‚Äì24 percentage points (tier constants vs reality)
[scripts/trigger-check.js:908‚Äì915](scripts/trigger-check.js:908) hardcodes:

| Setup | Published | Actual (confirmed) | Trades |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| A ‚Äî Full Confluence | **70%** | **86.1%** | 72 |
| B ‚Äî Partial Confluence | **60%** | **73.9%** | 257 |
| C ‚Äî Low Confluence | **48%** | **62.7%** | 225 |

Probabilities are anchored to old `smc-setups.md` estimates that the data has long since superseded. Every Discord signal alert understates the actual edge by ~14pp.
**Fix:** recompute from MongoDB on a rolling 30-day window (or quarterly review), update the tier constants, and add a TODO marker so they don't drift again.

## High-severity issues

### H1 ‚Äî OI factor is uninformative for shorts, *anti-correlated* for longs
[scripts/trigger-check.js:799‚Äì808](scripts/trigger-check.js:799) ‚Äî criterion #6. Pass-rule: long ‚áí `oiRising === true`, short ‚áí `oiRising === false`.
**Data (factor pass/fail √ó direction):**

| Direction | oi=true (rising for long, flat/falling for short) | oi=false |
|---|---|---|
| long  | n=105, **wr 70.5%, avgR 1.15** | n=87,  wr 77.0%, avgR 1.41 |
| short | n=55,  wr 61.8%, avgR 1.02 | n=26,  wr 61.5%, avgR 0.94 |

For longs, the "OI rising = conviction" check is **net negative** ‚Äî n=105 with OI rising win less often than n=87 with OI flat/falling. Plausible microstructure read: rising OI near a key support often signals a crowded long ‚Üí squeeze. For shorts the criterion is noise. The same issue surfaced in poly-btc-5 (see [refactors/poly-btc-5-remove-oi-factor.md](refactors/poly-btc-5-remove-oi-factor.md)).
**Fix:** either drop the OI criterion from BTC scoring, or invert it for longs, or split into "OI rising **at level**" + price-action filter.

### H2 ‚Äî Shorts underperform longs by 17pp; B+C shorts are barely profitable
| Direction √ó Setup | n | wr | avgR |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| long A | 72 | 86.1% | +1.25 |
| long B | 181 | 77.9% | +1.45 |
| long C | 131 | 67.2% | +1.17 |
| short B | 76 | 64.5% | +0.98 |
| short C | 94 | 56.4% | **+0.85** |

`short C ‚Äî Low Confluence` is the weakest cell (94 trades). Could be paused outright until the regime changes, or sized down. Zone breakdown agrees: **short VAH (n=128) is 54.7% wr, +0.82 avgR** ‚Äî the worst zone √ó direction combination, and the dominant short setup.
**Fix:** add a hard skip for `direction === 'short' && setupType.startsWith('C')` (or just `score < threshold` once C5 is recalibrated). Re-check after a regime shift.

### H3 ‚Äî Cooldown is too short for same-zone failures
`COOLDOWN_MS = 1 hour` at [scripts/trigger-check.js:41](scripts/trigger-check.js:41). The three 2026-04-13 supply-zone stops fired at 19:40 / 20:50 / 22:00 ‚Äî gaps of ~70 min, technically outside the cooldown, all stopped. After a stop on a zone, the zone has already done its job and is no longer "fresh." The same-zone, same-direction cooldown should be longer (4‚Äì6h or until mitigation).
**Fix:** extend cooldown after a confirmed stop on the zone; the direction-aware bypass at [scripts/trigger-check.js:1050‚Äì1054](scripts/trigger-check.js:1050) shows the cooldown logic already has hooks for outcome-awareness.

### H4 ‚Äî Performance is degrading over time
First half (2026-04-13 ‚Üí 05-04, n=279): **wr 73.8%, avgR +1.27**.
Second half (05-04 ‚Üí 05-14, n=280): **wr 67.1%, avgR +1.08**.
Could be regime shift, alpha decay, or overfit to early-window microstructure. Worth tracking weekly and recalibrating C5 quarterly.

## Medium-severity issues

### M1 ‚Äî `weeklyTrend` factor never registers `false` in 559 trades
389 null (neutral / unavailable), 170 true (with-trend), **0 false (counter-trend)**. Either the system never fires counter-trend (which would be a hidden filter ‚Äî worth confirming intentional) or `analyseWeeklyTrend()` rarely returns a non-neutral verdict that contradicts the signal. Without any `false` samples, the factor adds no information.
**Action:** instrument the function to log its raw result over a week and confirm the distribution.

### M2 ‚Äî `extractBTCFactors` substring matching is brittle
[scripts/migrate/import-trades.js:15‚Äì27](scripts/migrate/import-trades.js:15). `find('OI')` matches both "OI rising ‚Äî conviction" and "OI flat ‚Äî no directional conviction" (first hit wins). `find('CVD')` matches "CVD" in any label. Fine today, breakage waiting for any criterion rename.
**Fix:** tag each criterion with a stable `kind` field at creation time ([scripts/trigger-check.js:740‚Äì856](scripts/trigger-check.js:740)) and key the factor extractor off `kind`, not `label.includes(...)`.

### M3 ‚Äî `mtf-analyze.js` and `trigger-check.js` compute different probabilities
trigger-check: 5 hardcoded tier constants {48, 52, 60, 62, 70}.
mtf-analyze ([scripts/mtf-analyze.js:406‚Äì467](scripts/mtf-analyze.js:406)): weighted continuous formula clamped [28%, 91%].
`!analyze` outputs disagree with cron alerts on the same setup. Pick one model and reuse.

### M4 ‚Äî Two `B ‚Äî Reversal` and one `A ‚Äî Trend Continuation` legacy setupType values in data
Not produced by current code (`grep` of `scripts/` finds nothing). Zombie labels from a prior version. `B ‚Äî Reversal` is 3 trades, 0 wins, all stops ‚Äî the same 73773‚Äì73400 zone from C1.
**Action:** either purge from the dataset or relabel `setupType` during the next Mongo backfill so analytics doesn't trip over them.

### M5 ‚Äî Weekly-report "ALL SIGNALS" track includes unconfirmed phantom stops
[scripts/weekly-report.js:98](scripts/weekly-report.js:98). Once C1 is fixed this resolves automatically; until then it shaves ~1pp off the headline win rate.

### M6 ‚Äî No file lock on `.trigger-state.json` at 10-min cadence
`scripts/lib/lock.js` exists but is **not imported by `trigger-check.js`**. CDP polls occasionally exceed 10 min, and the next cron can mutate state mid-flight. Atomic rename writes don't help against read-modify-write races.
**Fix:** wrap the script body in `acquireLock('btc')`/`releaseLock()`.

### M7 ‚Äî `MAX_INVALIDATION_ALERTS = 3` cap is actually 6
[scripts/trigger-check.js:1136‚Äì1139](scripts/trigger-check.js:1136). Constant is named `MAX_INVALIDATION_ALERTS` but the check is `messages.length >= MAX_INVALIDATION_ALERTS * 2`. Just rename one or the other.
